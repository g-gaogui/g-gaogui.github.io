I am Gao Gui, a researcher and engineer working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and statistics.

My research interests include generative AI, multi-agent systems, probabilistic reasoning, and trustworthy AI. I am particularly interested in how statistical principles can improve the reliability, interpretability, and decision-making capabilities of modern AI systems. My recent work focuses on Bayesian approaches to multi-agent collaboration, belief-space reasoning, and the design of intelligent agent systems capable of operating in complex, uncertain environments.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, with a second major in Statistics and a minor in Mathematics at National University of Singapore. This interdisciplinary training has shaped my perspective that modern AI systems must be grounded not only in large-scale computation, but also in rigorous statistical reasoning to properly model uncertainty and support robust inference.

Alongside my academic interests, I have worked in industry and applied AI roles. I am currently an AI Data Scientist at UBS, where I work on machine learning systems and data-driven decision-making applications. I also serve as an Advisor at Transient AI and Director at Evolvable AI, where I contribute to AI system design, research direction, and applied development efforts. These experiences have strengthened my focus on bridging theoretical research with real-world intelligent systems.

My long-term goal is to develop AI systems that can reason, collaborate, and make reliable decisions under uncertainty, combining insights from statistics, machine learning, and multi-agent systems.

Feel free to explore my projects, research, and professional experience throughout this website.
