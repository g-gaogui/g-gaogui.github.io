<h2>Overview</h2>

<p>
The emergence of ChatGPT marked a major milestone in artificial intelligence,
demonstrating unprecedented capabilities in instruction following, reasoning,
and natural language interaction. This project examines Reinforcement Learning
from Human Feedback (RLHF), the core alignment technique underlying ChatGPT,
and analyzes how human feedback can be incorporated into the training process
to improve model helpfulness, harmlessness, and alignment with user intent.
The study further explores the limitations of current large language models and
discusses future research directions for building more reliable AI systems.
</p>

<h2>Abstract</h2>

<p>
This project investigates the role of Reinforcement Learning from Human
Feedback (RLHF) in enabling large language models to follow human instructions
and align their behavior with user preferences. The study reviews the
development of RLHF from earlier interactive reinforcement learning frameworks
such as TAMER to modern implementations in InstructGPT and ChatGPT. It
analyzes the training pipeline involving supervised fine-tuning, reward
modeling, and policy optimization using Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO).
Beyond the training process, the project examines key limitations of current
language models, including hallucination, reasoning failures, and
vulnerabilities to prompt injection, and discusses potential improvements
through multimodal learning and enhanced feedback-driven training.
</p>
