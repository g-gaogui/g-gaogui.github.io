<h2>Overview</h2>

<p>
Financial institutions increasingly rely on spoken interactions as a primary
source of client engagement and business intelligence. This project developed
an end-to-end multilingual speech intelligence platform capable of processing
English, Mandarin, and Cantonese conversations. The system integrates language
identification, speaker diarization, transcription, translation, and
context-aware question answering, enabling large-scale analysis of
advisor-client interactions across Singapore and Hong Kong markets.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Technical Approach</h2>

<p>
The system combines modern speech foundation models with downstream language
understanding components to transform raw audio into actionable knowledge.
Special emphasis was placed on adapting foundation models to domain-specific
financial conversations, where specialized terminology, multilingual speech,
and code-switching frequently challenge general-purpose speech recognition
systems. To improve model performance, synthetic data generation and domain
adaptation strategies were explored for fine-tuning Whisper v3 models.
</p>

<p>
A key aspect of the project was the development of a unified evaluation
framework that measures performance across multiple stages of the pipeline,
including language identification, speaker attribution, transcription quality,
translation accuracy, retrieval effectiveness, and question-answering
capabilities.
</p>

<h2>Evaluation Framework</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Language Identification:</strong> Confusion Matrix, Accuracy, F1 Score</li>

  <li><strong>Speaker Diarization:</strong> Diarization Error Rate (DER)</li>

  <li><strong>Speech Recognition:</strong> Word Error Rate (WER)</li>

  <li><strong>Translation:</strong> BLEU Score</li>

  <li><strong>Retrieval & Search:</strong> Precision, Recall, Retrieval Metrics</li>

  <li><strong>Question Answering:</strong> F1 Score, Answer Relevance Metrics</li>
</ul>
