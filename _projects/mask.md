<h2>Overview</h2>

<p>
The emergence of Disease X highlighted the need for reliable automated systems
capable of monitoring public health measures. This project investigates a
probabilistic approach to face mask detection by combining Bayesian
Convolutional Neural Networks (BCNNs) with Denoising Variational Autoencoders
(DVAEs). By explicitly modeling uncertainty, the proposed framework aims to
provide more reliable predictions than conventional deep learning approaches,
particularly in noisy and uncertain real-world environments.
</p>

<h2>Abstract</h2>

<p>
Traditional deep learning models often produce highly confident predictions
despite limited information or noisy inputs. To address this challenge, this
project explores a probabilistic framework for face mask detection that
quantifies predictive uncertainty alongside classification outcomes. A Bayesian
Convolutional Neural Network is employed to capture model uncertainty, while a
Denoising Variational Autoencoder is used to learn robust latent
representations from image data. Experimental evaluation demonstrates the
potential of Bayesian approaches to improve reliability and interpretability in
public health monitoring applications where prediction confidence is critical.
</p>
