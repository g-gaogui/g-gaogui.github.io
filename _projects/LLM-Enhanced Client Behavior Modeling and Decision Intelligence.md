<h2>Overview</h2>

<p>
Financial advisors must synthesize information from numerous fragmented data
sources to understand client needs and provide personalized recommendations.
This project developed a hybrid Bayesian–LLM decision intelligence platform
that combines transactional behavior, advisor interactions, market intelligence,
and external wealth data to build comprehensive client representations. The
resulting system supports advisor decision-making through interpretable client
profiling, behavioral modeling, and personalized recommendation generation.
</p>

<h2>Technical Approach</h2>

<p>
The platform combines probabilistic graphical models and Large Language Models
to capture both structured behavioral patterns and unstructured client context.
Bayesian networks were employed to model relationships among transactional
signals such as account flows, asset holdings, trading activities, and
engagement behavior. In parallel, speech-to-text systems and LLM pipelines
processed advisor-client conversations, meeting notes, and interaction history
to construct latent behavioral representations describing client preferences,
investment interests, and decision-making tendencies.
</p>

<p>
To enrich client understanding, the platform incorporated internal market
intelligence signals including market risk indicators, research insights, and
equity ratings. External wealth intelligence sources were further integrated to
provide additional context on client net worth, professional background,
interests, family office relationships, and social networks. These information
sources were fused into a unified client portrait that supports personalized
recommendation generation and advisor preparation workflows.
</p>
